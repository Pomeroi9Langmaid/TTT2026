EntryDocs detailed overview

Clear travel requirements, personalised for every journey.

EntryDocs helps travel companies show travellers exactly which visa, passport, health, transit and documentation requirements apply to their trip — before booking, after booking and before departure.

Why it matters

Travel requirements are one of the most confusing parts of international travel. Travellers often have to interpret fragmented government pages, search results or generic advice when they need a clear answer for their own itinerary.

What EntryDocs does

EntryDocs provides structured, rule-based travel requirement information that is continuously monitored, human-validated and personalised to the traveller and itinerary.

Booking hesitationUnclear requirements can make travellers delay or abandon a trip.
Support pressureCustomer teams receive repetitive “what documents do I need?” queries.
Operational riskWrong assumptions can create disruption before departure or at the airport.
Inconsistent messagingWeb, app, emails and support teams can tell travellers different things.

How EntryDocs works

EntryDocs converts traveller and itinerary data into clear requirements for the full journey, including stopovers and transit conditions.

EU Package Travel Directive context

For package travel organisers, retailers and travel platforms, clearer pre-travel information is becoming increasingly important. Travellers need to understand the documentation, visa, health and transit requirements that may affect their journey.

EntryDocs supports this by providing maintained, traveller-specific guidance that can be displayed before booking, after booking and before departure.

Helps present key travel requirements clearly and in good time
Supports consistent communication across web, app, email and support channels
Reduces reliance on generic, unstructured or outdated information
Useful for package, multi-leg and international journeys
Important: EntryDocs supports traveller-information transparency and operational readiness. It does not replace a company’s own legal assessment or formal compliance advice.
Practical point: the requirement information can be surfaced where travellers actually need it — not hidden in static help pages or left to customer support after the booking.
