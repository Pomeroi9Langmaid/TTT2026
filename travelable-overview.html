What it does

It transforms fragmented accessibility information into a structured, guided experience that helps passengers understand what support is available, what they need to prepare and what will happen at each journey stage.

Why it matters

For travellers with accessibility needs, choosing a flight is not just about price or schedule. It is about knowing whether the journey will work for them, how assistance is requested and confirmed, and what happens at the airport, onboard and on arrival.

Airline-first focus

At this phase, TravelAble is specifically focused on airline use cases, with the model designed to evolve for airports and other operators.

Passenger-specific guidance

Adapt information by route, airline, airports, mobility equipment, battery type, care needs and assistance requirements.

Step-by-step journey

Explain booking assistance, check-in, security, boarding, in-flight support, arrival and equipment handover.

Structured accessibility model

Centralise airline policies, airport procedures, aircraft rules and passenger-specific needs.

Operational alignment

Reduce uncertainty and help align digital communication with ground handling processes.

Scalable framework

Built to expand across routes, markets and experiences over time.

Business value

  • Clearer expectations before booking and before travel.
  • Fewer repetitive accessibility and PRM queries.
  • Reduced pre-trip anxiety for passengers.
  • Better alignment between customer experience, operations, digital product and ESG/accessibility goals.
